RGB LEDs

  • datasheet
  • digikey.ca

I have tested the RGB LED and it works nicely.

I used 200 ohm resistors so had about 10 mA for the green and blue and about 15 ma for the red. The intensity of this LED is much less than some of the hi efficiency ones we have. It is physically arger and frosted white in appearance and the colors are well combined.

One possible issue is the only distinction between the different leads is the lead length and that indication would disappear if/when the leads are trimed. One solution might be to mark the underside with a blue sharpie and a red sharpie. At the moment I have a blue sharpie and it appears that the white plastic takes the blue quite well. The order of the leads is

red—common Cathode—green— blue

Note there is an anode lead for each color.
